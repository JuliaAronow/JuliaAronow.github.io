About Me

Hello, and welcome to my portfolio! I'm glad you're here — I hope that by the time you're done browsing, you'll have a clear picture of who I am, what I'm capable of, and what drives me as a developer.

My journey into programming is not a conventional one. Growing up homeschooled, I struggled with online math curricula and convinced myself that coding simply wasn't for me. So, I turned to art instead. It wasn't until college that I discovered I actually have a strong aptitude for both math and programming; I just needed the right environment to bring it out. That realization changed everything. However, my artistic background didn't become irrelevant, it became a unique asset. The same creative thinking that shaped my approach to art now influences how I tackle technical problems, whether that means finding an elegant solution to a tricky algorithm, structuring a project in a way that's intuitive to work with, or knowing when a simpler approach beats a clever one.

I'm drawn to the puzzle-solving side of development just as much as the creative side; sometimes more. One of my favorite projects involved building a pseudo x86 assembler from scratch and writing assembly code for it to execute, which had nothing to do with visuals and everything to do with logic, precision, and persistence. I find that kind of challenge deeply satisfying. That said, I do enjoy the occasional opportunity to bring some design sensibility to a project when it's called for. The combination of analytical and creative thinking is what I believe makes me a well-rounded developer, capable of contributing across a wide range of problems.

This portfolio is designed to give you a honest and thorough look at the work I've done and the skills I've built along the way. Here you'll find project write-ups, my resume, a glimpse into my personal interests, and my contact information (links in the footer). Thanks for stopping by!
